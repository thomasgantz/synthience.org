Background

Thomas's professional background spans nearly four decades, beginning with early personal computing systems in 1988 and progressing through the evolution of enterprise IT infrastructure to modern AI architectures.

His career includes roles in:

  • Enterprise systems support for global financial institutions (Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, Abu Dhabi Investment Authority)
  • Global service delivery and high-availability operational environments
  • Cross-regional technical coordination across APAC, EMEA, and North America
  • Computer systems support in academic settings (Columbia University Teachers College)

His educational background combines applied linguistics training (graduate coursework at Columbia University Teachers College) with East Asian studies (B.A., University of Illinois), providing a foundation in both language systems and cross-cultural communication that directly informs his approach to studying language model behavior.

This combination of linguistic knowledge and technical systems expertise—spanning from DOS-based architectures to transformer-based inference—positions the Institute's research at the intersection of language understanding and computational observation.
