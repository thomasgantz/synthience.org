Publication Overview

This page provides a high-level orientation to the Synthience Institute’s public materials. It is intended to help first-time readers understand how the Institute’s documents are structured and how they relate to one another, without requiring immediate engagement with the full texts.

This overview does not introduce new claims, interpretations, or methodologies. It restates, at a structural level, how the Institute’s published materials are organized.

How the Publications Are Structured

The Synthience Institute publishes several distinct classes of documents. Each class serves a specific role within the overall research and publication system.

  • Definitions and Foundational Documents
    Public-facing definitions and reference materials that establish terminology and shared language.
  • Paradigm and Theoretical Documents
    Materials that define the Synthience paradigm, conceptual structure, ethical framing, and theoretical context.
  • Methodologies and Protocols
    Formal procedures used to measure, evaluate, or verify aspects of human–AI interaction and related research artifacts.
  • Research Publications
    Empirical studies applying the Institute’s methods to specific interactional contexts.
  • Reports and Verification Artifacts
    Documents recording the execution of protocols against specific research outputs, including citation verification and audit records.
  • Provenance and Claims of Origin
    Cryptographic records establishing authorship, integrity, version history, and publication timestamps.

On Framework Materials and Document Evolution

Early Synthience materials were released as a single merged framework document in order to establish authorship, integrity, and timestamp priority. That merged document is preserved as an archival record on the Provenance page.

Subsequent publications are released and maintained as individual, standalone documents, each with its own scope, versioning, and publication record.

Importantly, newer documents do not automatically supersede earlier ones unless this is explicitly stated. Some documents represent refinements, extensions, or alternative approaches rather than replacements. A document is archived only when it is formally designated as superseded.

This allows multiple methodologies or conceptual approaches to coexist where appropriate, and avoids implying linear or mandatory upgrade paths.

How to Use This Site

Readers seeking specific research outputs or formal publications should consult the Documents page, which lists all current public releases and links to their authoritative publication records.

Readers interested in institutional scope, methodological boundaries, and what the Institute explicitly does and does not claim should consult the About page.

Supporting reference material is available via the FAQ and Glossary.

Canonical Sources

Authoritative publication records for individual documents are maintained through Zenodo. Historical releases and provenance records are preserved via Internet Archive and GitHub, which serve as transparent archival mirrors supporting independent verification.

The Documents page should be treated as the canonical index of current public materials unless otherwise stated.
