SF037 — Citation Verification Protocol (CVP)

Overview notice: This page provides a high-level explanatory summary of the SF037 protocol. It is not the authoritative or citable artifact. The official publication is available on Zenodo.
Document system: Documents · Methodologies & Protocols

The Citation Verification Protocol (CVP) defines a structured methodology for verifying the integrity of citations used in AI-assisted research.

Purpose

CVP addresses a critical failure mode in AI-assisted scholarship: the propagation of citations that are nonexistent, misrepresented, or insufficiently supportive of the claims they accompany.

What CVP Does

  • Confirms the existence and accessibility of cited sources
  • Evaluates claim-level citation support
  • Identifies common AI citation failure modes
  • Produces structured verification artifacts

What CVP Does Not Do

  • Replace human scholarly judgment
  • Certify truth or factual correctness
  • Assume internal model comprehension

Relationship to Other Documents

CVP is applied directly in the verification of citations used in the SR001 RICO research paper, with results documented in a dedicated verification report.

For the complete protocol specification and version history, consult the official Zenodo publication.
