SF037 — Citation Verification Protocol (CVP)

Overview notice: This page provides a high-level explanatory summary of the SF037 document. It is not the authoritative or citable artifact. The official, versioned publication is available on Zenodo.
Document system: Documents · Methodologies & Protocols

The Citation Verification Protocol (CVP) is a general-purpose methodological framework for verifying the integrity of academic citations in AI-assisted research workflows.

Purpose

CVP addresses a critical failure mode in AI-assisted scholarship: the unverified propagation of citations that may be nonexistent, misrepresented, or only weakly supportive of the claims they are cited for.

Rather than relying on model self-attestation, CVP defines a structured, auditable verification process that can be applied across disciplines and publication types.

What CVP Does

  • Confirms the existence and accessibility of cited sources
  • Evaluates whether a source substantively supports the cited claim
  • Detects common AI citation failure modes
  • Produces a human-reviewable verification report

What CVP Does Not Do

  • Replace human scholarly judgment
  • Claim epistemic authority over research conclusions
  • Assume internal model comprehension or intent

The protocol is explicitly designed to augment, not automate, academic rigor.

Position Within the Synthience Framework

CVP functions as a methodological support tool within the broader Synthience research program. It is used directly in the verification of citations for primary research artifacts such as SR001 — RICO, with results documented in corresponding verification reports.

The protocol is platform-agnostic and may be applied to any AI-assisted research workflow where citation integrity matters.
