SF037 — Citation Verification Protocol (CVP)

Overview notice: This page provides a high-level summary of the SF037 protocol. It is not the full specification and should not be cited as the protocol itself. The authoritative, citable document is published on Zenodo.
Document system: Documents · Methodologies & Protocols

The Citation Verification Protocol (CVP) defines a structured, AI-assisted methodology for verifying academic citations used in research artifacts.

It addresses a common failure mode in AI-assisted research workflows: the unverified inclusion of sources that are nonexistent, irrelevant, or misrepresented.

Purpose and scope

CVP establishes an external, auditable process for confirming that citations exist, are accessible, and substantively support the claims for which they are cited.

The protocol is domain-agnostic and designed for use across academic disciplines.

Core principles

  • Verification over self-assertion
  • Claim-level citation validation
  • Separation of verification from authorship
  • Human-supervised adjudication

What CVP does not claim

  • It does not replace human scholarly judgment
  • It does not certify truth, only citation support
  • It does not infer author intent or interpretation

Relationship to other documents

CVP is applied directly in the verification of citations used in the SR001 RICO paper. The resulting verification artifact is published as a separate report.

Researchers, reviewers, and practitioners are encouraged to consult the full Zenodo publication for protocol details, usage constraints, and formal definitions.
