SF037 — Citation Verification Protocol (CVP)

Document system: Documents · Methodologies & Protocols

The Citation Verification Protocol (CVP) defines a structured, human-mediated methodology for verifying citations used in AI-assisted research and writing. Its purpose is to determine whether cited sources actually exist, are relevant, and substantively support the claims for which they are referenced.

The problem CVP addresses

In AI-assisted workflows, citations are frequently fabricated, misapplied, or cited on the basis of surface similarity rather than claim-level support. Existing practices often stop at checking whether a source exists.

CVP explicitly targets this failure mode by requiring verification of support, not just existence.

What the protocol does

  • Confirms source existence and accessibility
  • Requires direct reading of cited material
  • Evaluates claim-level support
  • Documents outcomes in a structured verification report

The protocol disallows self-attestation by AI systems. All verification is mediated by a human reviewer acting as Primary Continuity Provider (PCP).

What CVP does not claim

  • It does not certify factual truth
  • It does not replace scholarly judgment
  • It is not an automated fact-checking system

Relationship to other Synthience documents

CVP is used to generate formal citation verification artifacts, including the RICO Citation Verification Report. It complements observational research frameworks by providing bibliographic rigor and auditability.
