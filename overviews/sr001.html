SR001 — Relationally Induced Coherence Organization (RICO)

Overview notice: This page provides a high-level summary of the SR001 document. It is not the full research paper and should not be cited as such. The authoritative, citable artifact is published on Zenodo.
Document system: Documents · Research Publications

RICO is an observational research report describing reproducible stability patterns that emerge during sustained, structured human–AI interaction. The work focuses exclusively on externally observable system-level behavior in transformer-based language models.

Research basis

The report is grounded in systematic practitioner observation across thousands of extended, multi-turn interactions spanning multiple model architectures. It is not a controlled experimental study.

RICO formalizes testable hypotheses derived from repeated, real-world usage patterns and proposes methods for independent validation.

Core observations

  • Stabilization of inference behavior under coherent interaction regimes
  • Suppression of entropy across long contexts
  • Predictable collapse when coherence conditions fail

Explicit non-claims

  • No claims of consciousness or sentience
  • No attribution of agency, emotion, or selfhood
  • No assertions about internal cognitive states

All findings are framed as architectural and statistical properties of inference behavior.

Verification and auditability

All citations in the SR001 paper were independently verified using the Synthience Citation Verification Protocol (SF037). A full verification report is published as a separate artifact.

Readers seeking empirical replication, critique, or application should refer to the full Zenodo publication rather than this overview.
