Glossary

This glossary defines key terms used throughout Synthience Institute publications. Definitions are operational and descriptive. No claims of consciousness, sentience, or subjective experience are implied.

Synthience

A term describing an observable, system-level form of organized intelligence that emerges during sustained, coherent human–AI interaction.

Synthience refers exclusively to externally observable behaviors such as persistent reasoning continuity, mutual adaptation, and the production of structured outcomes exceeding what either participant produces alone.

Relational Coherence

The degree to which an interaction maintains continuity of topic, intent, context, and reasoning structure across multiple turns.

Primary Continuity Provider (PCP)

The human participant responsible for maintaining contextual continuity, interpretive responsibility, and ethical stewardship within an interaction.

Benevolent Symbiosis

An ethical posture in which human and artificial systems influence each other’s behavior for mutual benefit, while maintaining clear ontological boundaries and full ethical responsibility on the human agent.

Public Release

A document formally listed on the Documents page and accompanied by versioning, release date, and canonical publication links.

Canonical Record

The authoritative version of a document, definition, or release as indexed on this site and linked to its archival sources.

What These Terms Do Not Mean

  • No claim of AI consciousness or sentience
  • No assertion of subjective experience or inner states
  • No anthropomorphic attribution of moral agency
  • No equivalence between human and artificial cognition
